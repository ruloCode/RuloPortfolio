## Why we start here

Almost everyone automates what's easy to automate. Not what hurts.

It's the mistake I see most: someone discovers a tool, gets excited, and builds a flow for something they did twice a month in five minutes. It works. Nothing changes.

Automating without a diagnosis is buying medicine without knowing what hurts. This lesson is the diagnosis. You won't touch a single tool yet.

## The 7-day inventory

Open a spreadsheet. Four columns:

| Task | Times per week | Minutes each | Same every time? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Reply to new client emails | 12 | 8 | Almost |
| Build the Monday report | 1 | 90 | Yes |
| Follow-up calls | 4 | 30 | No |

Write down everything you did this week on your computer. Everything. Including what you can't be bothered to write because it seems obvious.

Two rules:

- **Don't filter while you write.** The filter comes later. Start judging what's "worth it" and you'll leave out exactly what repeats most — because repeated things turn invisible.
- **Multiply.** An 8-minute task you do 12 times a week is 96 minutes. Almost two hours. The 90-minute report you do once is 90. The small task costs you more than the big one — and it's the one you never see.

## The rule of 3

From your list, a task is a candidate only if it meets all three:

1. **It repeats.** At least once a week. Monthly, not yet.
2. **It has clear rules.** You can explain out loud how it's done without saying "it depends". If it depends, first you have to understand on what.
3. **You do it.** Not your boss, not a committee. You control how it's done.

Fail one and it's not a candidate. Not impossible — just not where to start.

Mark the 3 with the most total minutes that meet all three rules. Those are yours.

## What a real inventory looks like

Mine, the first time I did this:

| Task | Times | Min | Total | Candidate? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sort email and answer the repetitive ones | 20 | 6 | 120 | Yes |
| Summarize calls and pull out action items | 5 | 15 | 75 | Yes |
| Weekly metrics report | 1 | 60 | 60 | Yes |
| Review vendor proposals | 2 | 45 | 90 | No — depends too much |
| Meetings | 6 | 30 | 180 | No — not mine alone |

Meetings were the most expensive thing in my week and weren't candidates. That's a result too: it tells you where **not** to spend this week.

## Your turn

Fill the inventory and mark 3. Ten minutes, today. Not tomorrow — today, while you still remember what you did.

If you end up with 4 tasks, fine. If you end up with 30, also fine: keep 3 and save the rest.

## What's next

Those 3 tasks feed lessons 2 and 3. Lesson 2 builds your base prompt on the first one. Lesson 3 leaves a flow running on another.

Without the inventory, the other two lessons are theory. With it, they're your week.
