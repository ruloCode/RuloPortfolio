## What a flow is, in three sentences

A flow has three parts: **trigger → step → result**.

Something happens (an email arrives). Something is done (AI summarizes it). The result goes somewhere (your team chat).

That's it. Everything else is a variation on the same thing.

## The tool

We'll use a visual flow tool — clicks, no code. There are several and they all do this equally well: Make, Zapier, n8n. Pick one and follow along.

And here's a warning I mean seriously: **don't marry any of them.** They'll change, they'll raise prices, better ones will show up. What you're learning isn't the tool — it's the pattern. The pattern outlives the tool.

## Today's flow

An email arrives → AI summarizes it and pulls the action items → it lands where you actually read it.

I picked this one because email was the candidate task in almost everyone's inventory. If yours was different, change the trigger and the rest is the same.

## Step by step

1. **Create the account** and start a new, blank flow.
2. **Trigger**: connect your email. Filter — not every email, only the ones that matter. A label, a sender, a subject. Don't filter and you'll automate noise.
3. **AI step**: add the AI module and paste your prompt from lesson 2, adapted: "summarize this email in 3 lines and list the concrete action items, if any". The email body goes in as context.
4. **Result**: send it where you'll actually read it. Your team chat, a document, an email to yourself. Where you already look. Not a new place you'll forget.
5. **Turn it on.**

## The test

Trigger it yourself. Send yourself an email that matches the filter and watch what happens.

**If you didn't see it run, it isn't running.** This isn't paranoia: half the flows people "build" never executed once. They were saved. Not the same thing.

## When it breaks

It will break. Not your fault: a format changes, a permission expires, a weird email arrives.

When it does:

- Look at the execution history. All these tools have it and it tells you exactly which step died.
- 80% of the time it's the connected account's permission. Reconnect it.
- If the AI step returns garbage, the problem is the context reaching it — not the model.

Knowing it will break and knowing where to look is the difference between having an automation and having a toy.

## Your turn

Leave it on. Close your laptop and it keeps working.

Tomorrow you check what ran. That's all.
